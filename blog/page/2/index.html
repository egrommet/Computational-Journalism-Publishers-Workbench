Computational Journalism Publishers Workbench Version 0.3.0 Released

Aug 19th, 2012 | Comments

I’ve just released version 0.3.0 of the Computational Journalism Publishers Workbench. This is mostly bug fixes and more packages are now installed from upstream binary or source rather than from the distributions. The only new package is the JabRef bibliography tool. And, as I noted yesterday, I am no longer releasing any Twitter tools as open source.

The short-term focus of the workbench is on tools for the creation of web pages and eBooks from clean data. There are many fine tools for acquiring and cleaning data, both open source and proprietary. I’ve kept Google Refine and the PDF extraction tools in the workbench because people ask for them, but they aren’t something I use on a regular basis. Neither are scrapers, for that matter.

Some really important stories have come from scrapers and PDF extraction, to be sure. But the kinds of stories that interest me most – finance, economics and the environment – come with copious cleaned datasets. If you’re interested in scrapers, I highly recommend Scraping for Journalists by Paul Bradshaw. Paul is a Professor of journalism in the UK and runs the Online Journalism Blog. And he’s on Twitter, of course – @paulbradshaw.
